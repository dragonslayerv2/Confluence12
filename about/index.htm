About Confluence


In geography, a confluence is the meeting of two or more bodies of water. It usually refers to the point where two streams flow together, merging into a single stream. But here Confluence is the Annual Cultural Fest of National Institute of Technology, Kurukshetra. Like the institute, the fest is also Best in India which every year witness more than 10,000 participants participating in about 100 Events. Confluence Features many events in the Field of Dance, Music, Dramatics, Finearts, Modeling, Photography, Literature and many more with Hefty Cash Prizes.



Theme


Many philosophies and worldviews have a set of classical elements believed to reflect the simpliest essential part and principles of which anything consists or upon which theconstitution and fundamental powers of anything are based. Most frequently, classical elements refers to ancient beliefs inspired by natural observation of the phases of matter.Historians trace the evolution of modern theory pertaining to the chemical elements as well as chemical compounds and mixtures of natural substance to Medieval, Islamic and Greek models. Many concepts once thought to be analogous, such as the Chinese Wu Xing, are now understood more figuratively.


The Four Elements Fire, Earth, Water and Air are related to each other as Follows-
   Air is primarily wet and secondarily hot.
   Fire is primarily hot and secondarily dry
   Earth is primarily dry and secondarily cold.
   Water is primarily cold and secondarily wet.


This year the 4 Elements is the Theme for Confluence which signifies the Confluence of Cultures and Traditions of different Elemental Components into One big Event to produce a whole new culture which the Land of Mahabharata will witness in February 2012.
